Sunken Ships (that aren't the Titanic)

The RMS Titanic was not the only White Star Line ship to sink and cause tremendous loss of life. Here are a few more ships that sank while sailing under their flag.

  1. RMS Tayleur
    • Date of Sinking: 21 January 1854
    • Location: Lambay Island, near Ireland
    • Cause: Ran aground due to compass issues caused by the ship having an iron hull, oops
    • Extra: Woe to this unfortunate ship, for she too sank on her maiden voyage.
  2. SS Atlantic
    • 1 April 1873
    • Halifax, Nova Scotia
    • Multiple miscommunication issues between the captain and the chief engineer about coal reserves, as well as none of the crewmates having any knowledge (except one, whose warnings went unheeded) of the treacherous waters off the coast of Halifax.
    • the White Star Line's biggest recorded loss of life (prior to the Titanic, that is).
  3. RMS Republic
    • 24 January 1909
    • Near Nantucket, Massachusetts.
    • Collided with the Italian liner SS Florida while sailing through a particularly thick fog.
    • Was known as the "Millionaire's Ship," or "palatial liner" during her career, because of the number of wealthy patrons who traveled by her.
  4. HMHS Britannic
    • 21 November 1916
    • near Kea, Aegean Sea
    • collided with a naval mine planted by a German U-boat during WW1
    • Built with lots of extra safety features (since the WSL didn't want her to suffer the same fate as her sister), this ship only lost 30 lives on the day of her sinking, despite taking 1/3 of the time to sink that Titanic had.
  5. Titan
    • 18 June 2023
    • Near the wreck of the RMS Titanic
    • Subpar construction issues which caused the sub to implode upon descent, while visiting the wreckage of the RMS Titanic.
